Analysis of Grades at UMD with and wihtout COVID-19¶

Auva Amirmokri, Josiah Kim, Jessica Llewellyn

INTRODUCTION

The overall goal of our project is to analyze if there is a change in grades over 2 time periods at the University of Maryland, without covid (pre Spring 2020) and with covid (Spring 2020 and after). Through our analysis we are hoping to find if there is a trend between grades and time period. More specifically, we will be analyzing 4 different departments, one we deem the easiest, one we deem the hardest and one we deem average. We will also be analyzing the CMNS data to see how it compares to the other 3 departments. Through our analysis we want to see if grades fell more for the easier department or for the harder department or if grades stayed the same. As a group, we think that the grades for the harder department will drop more because the department is already challenging so adding online zoom lectures, discussions and tests to the equation might make it even more challenging

REQUIRED TOOLS

We will need the following libraries for this project:

  1. requests
  2. pandas
  3. matplotlib
  4. Seaborn

1. Data Collection: The first phase of our analysis was the data collection. After looking through many online datasets, we could not find one that suited our needs/had enough data. We then thought to look at the data given by Planet Terp since their data is very relevant in our lives. Luckily, Planet Terp is an extensive, well documented dataset so it was relatively easy to collect data from it.

Planet Terp has their data divided by courses, professors, grades, searching for an individual class/professor, and schemas. Their API is so well documented that it also gives examples of how to make data requests.

With all this information, we specifically wanted the the "grades" data. On Planet Terp, the grade data is given by section and by semester for each course so it was easy to extract per department, per semester. Before we got to this point however, we tried extracting other data to see how the extraction from the website is formatted. Below we made an API request to Planent Terp and got the output in 2 ways, one of which was .json and the other was a dataframe. Through this test we were able to see that the second choice formatted the data better for us to view and analyze.

When we were collecting the data, we realized it was a lot. Therefore, we wanted to simplify and not overwhelm our findings by only looking at a few departments rather than all of them. To do this we did a simple google search of the easiest and hardest departments at UMD and it brough us to this Planet Terp page: https://planetterp.com/tools/coursedifficulty. This page was not talked about in the API however it was taken from the same website. From this page we specifically looked at the "Difficulty by department" section in order to come up with the 3 departments we thought were the easiest, hardest and one in the middle. At the top of the list we found the easiest department (easiest because of highest GPA). We did not take the first department however because we wanted a department that was associated with a real major (for example CPET is a series of scholars classes rather than a major) and we did the same for the bottom. For the middle department we took a look and decided BMGT would be a good middle that we were also curious about.

# given a dataframe, adds a column of the average gpa as a number and a column for the total students in the class
def addGrades(df):
  avgGpas = []
  classSize = []

  for index, row in df.iterrows():
    gpaSum = (row['A+'] + row['A'])*4 + row['A-']*3.7 + row['B+']*3.3 + row['B']*3.0 + row['B-']*2.7
    gpaSum += row['C+']*2.3 + row['C']*2.0 + row['C-']*1.7 + row['D+']*1.3 + row['D']*1.0 + row['D-']*.7

    numStudents = row['A+'] + row['A'] + row['A-'] + row['B+'] + row['B'] + row['B-'] + row['C+'] + row['C'] + row['C-'] + row['D+'] + row['D'] + row['D-'] + row['F'] + row['W']
    classSize.append(numStudents + row['Other'])
    if numStudents == 0:
      avgGpas.append(-1)
    else:
      avgGpas.append(gpaSum / numStudents)
    
  df['avgGpa'] = avgGpas
  df['classSize'] = classSize

  # drops classes with average gpas over 4 or less than 0
  df = df.drop(df[(df.avgGpa < 0) & (df.avgGpa > 4)].index, inplace = True)

# given a department string, returns a dataframe containing all the classes in that department sorted by semester
def getAllInDept(dept):
  toRet = pd.DataFrame()

  deptCourses = allCourses[allCourses['department'] == dept]

  for index, row in deptCourses.iterrows():
    site = requests.get('https://api.planetterp.com/v1/grades',
                        params = {'course': row['course']}, headers = headers)
    toAppend = pd.DataFrame.from_dict(site.json())
    toRet = toRet.append(toAppend, ignore_index=True)

  addGrades(toRet)

  # only want courses at the undergrad level
  identifier = dept+'1|'+dept + '2|' + dept +'3|' + dept+'4'
  toRet = toRet[toRet['course'].str.contains(identifier)]

  toRet = toRet.sort_values('semester')
  return toRet

# returns a dataframe of the average gpas of all the classes in df parameter over 4 different time frames
# also updates parameter df to contain a column indicating what timeframe it is in
# timeframe is with covid (full online learning) and passfail (202001/202101), covid no passfail (202108), pre-covid, and 
# blended learning post-covid (202108)
def splitByTimeframe(df, dept):

  gpas = [[0 for i in range(4)] for j in range(5)]
  numClasses = [[0 for i in range(4)] for j in range(5)]
  #numPF = [0,0,0,0]
  levels = [[],[],[],[],[]]
  timeframes = []

  for index, row in df.iterrows():

      levelIdx = -1

      # checks to see what level current course is
      if (dept + '1') in row['course']:
        levelIdx = 0
      
      elif (dept + '2') in row['course']:
        levelIdx = 1
      
      elif (dept + '3') in row['course']:
        levelIdx = 2

      # level 400
      else:
        levelIdx = 3

      i = 3

      # covid 
      if row['semester'] == '202001' or row['semester'] == '202101':
        i = 0
        timeframes.append('Online w/PF')

      # covid no passfail
      elif row['semester'] == '202008':
        i = 1
        timeframes.append('Online w/oPF')

      elif row['semester'] == '202108':
        i = 2
        timeframes.append('Post-Covid')

      else:
        timeframes.append('In-Person')

      gpas[levelIdx][i] += row['avgGpa']
      numClasses[levelIdx][i]+= 1

      # updates total gpa
      gpas[4][i] += row['avgGpa']
      numClasses[4][i]+= 1

  for i in range(0,5) :
    for j in range(0,4):
      gpas[i][j] = gpas[i][j] / numClasses[i][j]

  byEra = pd.DataFrame()

  byEra['timeframe'] = ['Online w/PF', 'Online w/oPF', 'Post-Covid', 'In-Person']
  byEra['1xxAvgGpa'] = gpas[0]
  byEra['2xxAvgGpa'] = gpas[1]
  byEra['3xxAvgGpa'] = gpas[2]
  byEra['4xxAvgGpa'] = gpas[3]
  byEra['Overall'] = gpas[4]

  #creates a new column in original dataframe to determine which timeframe a course was in
  df['timeframe'] = timeframes

  return byEra

4. Hypothesis testing

Upon diving into our various datasets, we have been able to see how the COVID-19 pandemic has affected academics.

As we mentioned earlier, before starting the project we believed that the grades for the harder department will drop more because the department is already challenging.

Therefore, our null hypothesis in this project is that student’s grades in the hardest departments were NOT affected more than the other departments due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Our alternative hypothesis is that student’s grades in the hardest department are affected the most from COVID out of the 4 departments we looked at.

We further believe that grades in the hardest department worsened the most at the start of the pandemic, but as students got more and more adjusted to various types of learning (such as online and blended), their grades would get slightly better. Now that classes are back in-person for the most part, we suspect that grades are doing much better than they were at the start of COVID-19.

5.Insight

We discovered that

  • The Math department had a more massive increase in their GPA after COVID began, but a more evenly spread out distribution of GPAs
  • The Music department had a stable GPA throughout the pandemic. The Business school is a very close second
  • The Business school had a pretty even distribution of grades before and during the pandemic

The Math department had the lowest GPA before COVID, and upon switching to online learning, the median of GPAs increased drastically and overtime it they are going back to normal. This can definitely be attributed to the addition of a Pass/Fail option, as the massive jump in average GPA happened right at that time.

The Music department had minimal changes throughout the pandemic, which can be surprising as classes where you have to play an instrument are much easier to teach and participate in when you are in-person.

As for the Business department, we see that it too had no drastic changes before/after COVID-19 started. Its current GPA distribution is almost identical to the pre-COVID GPA distribution.

The CS department, similar to the Math department, saw a pretty sharp increase in GPA when Pass/Fail was introduced as a result of COVID. Overtime, however, the GPAs started to fall back to normal.

Therefore, we can conclude that although some departments are harder to teach online, the addition of a Pass/Fail option and other exceptions being made during the pandemic may have aided students and boosted their GPAs.

We can only speculate that with online learning there was an uptick in cheating, which is why many professors made their exams harder but open-note.

It is well-known that many people have been suffering with anxiety and other mental illnesses as a result of the pandemic. This may have had very negative impacts on students as well, however the approach that these departments took helped aid their students to do better as opposed to fail.
